Ole Gunnar Solskjaer at the wheel

The impact of Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has been clear to see at Manchester United. An excellent start under the Norwegian coach have seen spirits revitalised at Old Trafford as United keep their hopes of top-four football alive. And now supporters have been airing a new chant to document feeling good factor since Solskjaer took over at the club. Although the song began to first circulate earlier in the season when Jose Mourinho was in charge, the new chant has gained a new cult following after it was adapted to praise Solskjaer. The original chant first gathered mass popularity when it was chanted by joyous fans following the incredible Champions League victory away against Juventus last year. However, while the new version of the fan chant still pays tribute to Mourinho signings Alexis Sanchez, Paul Pogba and Fred. There is also lines which declare a love for Marcus Rashford and the new manager in charge of the club.

Rashford not happy about results

Manchester United striker Marcus Rashford has said he didn't think Liverpool "were good enough" to deserve a point following their 1-1 draw at Old Trafford on Sunday. Rashford, 21, opened the scoring with his fourth goal of the season, and substitute Adam Lallana equalised for Liverpool in the 85th minute after the visitors enjoyed a much-improved second half. United's frontman spoke to Sky Sports (h/t Goal) after full time and said: "We deserved the three points. I don't think they were good enough to get a draw out of the game or a win but this is football. It was a big opportunity missed and we just fell short. Hopefully in midweek we can get a win and it will start a run for us." Sunday's result was United's sixth in a row without a win (in 90 minutes), though Rashford sensed the positives to take from a point against the Premier League leaders.
